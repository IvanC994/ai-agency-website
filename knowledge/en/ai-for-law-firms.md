WHERE BILLABLE HOURS DISAPPEAR

## Law is knowledge and judgment. Everything around it does not have to remain manual.

The goal is not to make AI a lawyer. It is to remove repetition, searching, and preparation so lawyers spend more time on strategy, negotiation, and advice.

01

### The first draft always starts from zero

A contract, filing, or client reply is assembled by copying from old matters. An hour goes to work AI can prepare for review.

02

### Emails and opinions consume billable time

Questions repeat, while formatting, cleanup, and tone adaptation consume time the client does not recognize as legal value.

03

### Knowledge remains locked inside matters

A conclusion from a similar matter is known only to the person who worked on it. Searching the archive can take longer than rewriting.

04

### Junior onboarding takes months

A junior learns templates, precedents, and the firm’s way of working by consuming senior time, although many answers already exist in internal documents.

CONFIDENTIALITY BEFORE PRODUCTIVITY

## AI enters as a controlled internal tool, not an open window into client data.

Before selecting a model, we map data types, professional duties, purpose, permitted users, retention, and processor contracts. The setup is chosen so client data is not used to train public models, and every exception must be known and approved.

### Allowed and prohibited

A clear matrix of data and tasks that may or may not enter an AI workflow.

### Role-based access

Users see only the matters, sources, and functions required for their work.

### Sources and audit trail

Responses show the documents used, while important actions remain logged.

### Retention and deletion

Retention, logs, copies, and deletion are defined before production, not afterwards.

!

### AI does not provide independent legal advice.

The system prepares drafts, extracts data, compares versions, and finds material. A responsible lawyer checks facts, law, strategy, and final wording — like a senior reviewing the work of a junior who never signs alone.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

## What law firms want to know.

What about client confidentiality?+

It is the first project topic. We define what may enter the system, choose a setup where data is not used to train public models, control access, retention, and deletion, and build the rules into team training.

Does AI write legal advice?+

Not independently. AI prepares a draft and finds material, while the lawyer checks facts and law, exercises judgment, approves the text, and signs.

Our team has no technical background. Is that a problem?+

No. Workshops use practical examples and clear procedures, so users do not need to understand the model’s technical internals to use the tool responsibly.

Where do we start?+

With a snapshot of where non-billable hours go. A common first step is a usage policy, training, and document preparation, followed by internal search and connected automation.

How much does bringing AI into a law firm cost?+

It depends on users, data types, integrations, and use cases. Training is the smallest and common first step; an internal assistant and automation are introduced in phases after a clear proposal.

Does AI work with documents in Serbian?+

Yes. Systems can work in English and Serbian, including legal terminology, but quality must be tested against your templates, style, and typical documents.
