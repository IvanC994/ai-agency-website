WHEN CUSTOM MAKES SENSE

## We do not build custom because it sounds advanced.

A custom approach is justified when an off-the-shelf tool creates more compromise than value and the unique process genuinely matters to the business.

01

### Knowledge is fragmented and specific

Important answers live across documents, email, CRM, and a few experts, while a generic chatbot lacks the required context.

02

### The process has many rules and exceptions

A decision depends on role, client, status, document, or a combination of data that a standard tool does not understand.

03

### Multiple systems must work as one

Data needs to be read, validated, and written across CRM, ERP, calendars, documents, and internal software.

04

### Control, privacy, or audit are essential

You need to know which source was used, who approved an action, and what happened at each step.

WHAT WE CAN BUILD

## AI that becomes part of the operation, not another tab.

The system can begin with one focused module and gradually become a connected operational layer.

01

### Internal knowledge copilot

Answers employees from approved procedures, contracts, price lists, and technical documentation, with a link or citation to the source.

Knowledge is available without searching for the one person who knows everything.

02

### Intelligent document processing

Classifies documents, extracts relevant fields, checks them against rules, and prepares the next task or draft.

Less manual retyping and easier control of exceptions.

03

### Case intake and routing

Collects information, checks completeness, determines category, and routes a case to the right person with a structured summary.

Every case begins with context, ownership, and priority.

04

### Decision-support system

Combines data and rules, presents options, risks, and reasoning, while the responsible person makes the final important decision.

Faster, more consistent assessment without hidden automated decisions.

05

### AI workflow orchestration

Monitors status, calls tools, waits for approval, processes responses, and moves work across multiple systems.

Complex work advances without manually chasing each step.

06

### Custom portal or workspace

A focused interface where employees or clients see relevant data, interact with AI, and complete tasks according to their role.

Technology fits the work instead of forcing work into the interface.

BUILD OR BUY

## Custom is not always the right answer.

If an existing product solves the requirement reliably and economically, we will recommend and connect it. Building makes sense when process, data, control, or user experience are specific enough that standard tools keep creating manual workarounds.

### Use an existing tool when

-   ✓The process is standard
-   ✓Required integrations already exist
-   ✓Speed matters more than customization
-   ✓Tool limitations do not compromise the outcome

### Build custom when

-   ✓The process is a competitive advantage
-   ✓Rules and exceptions are specific
-   ✓Special data, roles, or audit are required
-   ✓Multiple systems must feel like one

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

## Before investing in custom AI.

What is a custom AI system?+

It is an application or workflow designed around your data, rules, user roles, and business outcome, instead of forcing the process into the capabilities of one standard product.

How do we know an existing tool is not enough?+

We first review the market and your current stack. If a product solves the problem reliably with acceptable cost and control, integration is often better than custom development.

How long does development take?+

A focused first production system often takes 4–8 weeks. Timing depends on data quality, integrations, security requirements, testing, and speed of business approvals.

How much does it cost?+

Pricing depends on architecture, data, integrations, interface, risk, and support. After discovery, you receive a clear scope, phases, development cost, and expected operating costs.

Do we need to replace current software?+

Not necessarily. A custom layer is often built above existing CRM, ERP, documents, and internal systems. We recommend replacement only when the current tool blocks reliable integration.

Which AI model do you use?+

We choose based on task, language, quality, privacy, speed, and cost. Where practical, we design the architecture so core business logic is not permanently tied to one provider.

Will our data train a public model?+

That depends on the selected service and contractual settings. Before implementation, we review provider policies and choose a configuration aligned with project requirements; we do not assume every AI service behaves the same way.

How do you prevent incorrect answers?+

We use approved sources, validation, confidence thresholds, explicit restrictions, source citations, human approval, and realistic scenario tests. For important decisions, AI prepares a recommendation and a responsible person approves it.

What do we receive after launch?+

You receive the production system in the agreed scope, documented workflows and integrations, user training, metrics, and a plan for maintenance or further development.
